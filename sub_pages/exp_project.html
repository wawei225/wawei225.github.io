A/B Testing Project

Introduction

This project is a simulation designed to showcase a common A/B testing process that is typically conducted in e-commerce and technology companies. A/B testing is a fundamental method used to validate design changes, optimize user experience, and improve key business metrics, such as conversion rates. The goal of this experiment is to evaluate the impact of a redesigned trending section on the homepage of our website.

Problem Statement

The trending section on our homepage displays items that have received the highest number of orders on our platform. However, data analysis has shown that the conversion rate for this section is lower than the average conversion rate across the homepage. This suggests that the current design of the trending section may not be sufficiently attractive or engaging for users. The product team hypothesizes that a new UI design will enhance visibility and usability, ultimately increasing the number of users who click the items on the trending section (click-through rate).

Scope of the experiment

To simplify the scope of this A/B test, the website will be structured into two main pages and their key sections:

  • Homepage
    • Discovery Section: General recommendations based on user preferences. This section displays multiple different items and users can click on any of them, which will direct them to the item page.
    • Trending Section: Items with the highest order volume on the platform, and users can click on any of them, which will direct them to the item page.
  • Item Page
    • Description Section: Product details and specifications.
    • Review Section: User-generated reviews and ratings.
